The Lost Star

High above the clouds, in the Kingdom of the Sky, lived tiny twinkling stars. Every night, they sparkled brightly, lighting up the world. But one little star, named Lina, had a problem—she wasn’t as bright as the others.

“I wish I could shine like you all,” Lina sighed.

The other stars tried to cheer her up. “You are special in your own way, Lina,” said the biggest star. But Lina still felt small and dim.

One night, while playing with a comet, Lina spun too fast—whoosh! She slipped and fell from the sky!

Down, down, down she went until—plop!—she landed softly in a little garden. She blinked her tiny light and looked around. The sky seemed so far away.

A little boy named Kian was playing outside when he saw something glowing in the grass.

“Wow! A real star!” Kian gasped. He carefully picked her up. “Are you okay?”

Lina nodded sadly. “But I lost my way home.”

Kian wanted to help. “Don’t worry, we’ll get you back to the sky!”

They tried many ways—jumping high, climbing a tree, and even tying balloons to Lina! But nothing worked.

Then Kian had an idea. “Maybe you just need to shine brighter!”

He took Lina to the highest hilltop, where the sky was the clearest. “Close your eyes and believe in yourself,” he whispered.

Lina thought about all the happy moments—the warmth of the sky, the laughter of her star friends, and now, Kian’s kindness.

And then… she began to glow! Brighter and brighter until—whoosh!—she shot back up into the sky!

That night, Kian looked up and saw a new, extra-bright star twinkling down at him.

Lina had found her way home. But she never forgot her friend on Earth, shining her brightest just for him.
